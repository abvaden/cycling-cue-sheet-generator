## Design Context

### Users
Enthusiast cyclists preparing compact, glanceable route references for personal rides, races, and events. They work on desktop or tablet before riding and need the printed result to fit their exact top-tube or handlebar geometry.

### Brand Personality
Precise, purposeful, and performance-minded. The interface should create confidence that distances, layouts, and print dimensions are trustworthy.

### Aesthetic Direction
A light technical-editorial workspace inspired by route books and race ephemera: warm paper, dark ink, hairline rules, and racing-red signals. Avoid generic dashboard cards, decorative gradients, glass effects, and novelty cycling imagery.

### Design Principles
- Treat the printable sheet as the primary artifact; controls support it rather than compete with it.
- Make physical size, route distance, and overflow status continuously visible.
- Use progressive disclosure so importing and adding the first cue stays simple.
- Keep critical ride information legible at a glance and never rely on color alone.
- Preserve manual arrangement while providing reliable distance-ordered defaults.
